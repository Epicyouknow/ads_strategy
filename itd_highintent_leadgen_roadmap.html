High-Intent, High-Interest Targeting
At this budget, every rupee must hit someone who is actively in buying mode — not casually curious. Here's exactly what "high intent" means and how to find it on Meta.
What "High Intent" means for a B2B service ad on Meta

A high-intent prospect on Meta has already: (1) researched agencies or performance marketing, (2) experienced pain with their current setup — rising CAC, flat ROAS, or broken retention, (3) has budget authority (founder/CMO), and (4) is in a business lifecycle moment — launching, scaling past ₹1Cr ARR, or switching agencies. Your targeting must stack signals to find this person, not scatter broadly.

Critical rule: Never run broad at ₹300–500/day

Broad targeting and Advantage+ audience need ₹1,000+/day to learn fast enough. At ₹300–500/day, always use manual interests with 2–3 layers AND geographic precision. The algorithm needs signal density — you provide it through tight stacking, not loose broadness. Exception: if you have 50+ past leads in your pixel, you can test 1 Advantage+ ad set at ₹80/day using those as a seed.

Campaign Architecture
Exact Meta Ads account structure for both ₹300/day and ₹500/day — campaigns, ad sets, bidding, and optimisation events.
WEEK 1 · DAY 1–7
Pixel, Audiences, Forms — Before a Single Ad Runs
Install pixel → verify events (Lead, PageView, WA click) → build all custom audiences → create lead forms (general + industry) → set up Calendly pixel event → create all ad creative library assets. Do not run a single lead ad until pixel shows 50+ PageView events from organic/direct traffic. Without this baseline, Meta has no signal to optimise from.
WEEK 1–2 · DAY 3–10
Warm-Up: Tiny Engagement Campaign (₹50/day, 7 days only)
Before launching lead gen: run a ₹50/day engagement campaign (boosted post or video view campaign) to D2C India interest audience, Pan India. Goal: 200+ video views and 30+ post engagements. This seeds your custom audiences AND gives Meta 7 days of behavioral data before your real spend begins. At Day 7, kill this campaign — it has served its purpose.
WEEK 2 · DAY 8–14
Campaign 1 Live: Lead Generation (Main Campaign)
Launch the primary lead gen campaign with 2 ad sets (₹300 plan) or 3 ad sets (₹500 plan). Use instant forms first — not landing page traffic. Reason: at low budgets, landing page conversion campaigns need 50 conversions/week to exit learning phase. Instant forms have higher CTR and lower barrier. Switch to landing page campaigns in Week 5 once you have conversion data.
WEEK 3 · DAY 15–21
First Optimisation + Retargeting Launch
After 7 days of lead gen: pause bottom 1 creative by CPL. Kill any ad set with 0 leads AND CPL over ₹1,500. Launch Campaign 2 (retargeting) with website visitors and video viewers. At ₹300/day: retargeting gets ₹100/day. At ₹500/day: retargeting gets ₹120/day.
WEEK 4–6 · DAY 22–45
Stabilisation + Creative Refresh + First Scale Decision
At Day 30: review CPL trend. If CPL is falling week-over-week and you have 10+ leads — you're in the learning improvement zone, do not change the campaign structure. Just refresh creatives (new hooks on same structure). If CPL is flat or rising: change audience stack, not budget. Scale budget only when CPL hits target for 5+ consecutive days.
MONTH 2+
Lookalike Layer + Budget Upgrade Decision
Once you have 50+ lead events: create 1% LAL from lead submitters. Add as 3rd ad set in Campaign 1. Budget upgrade from ₹300 → ₹500 is appropriate when: (1) CPL is stable under ₹600 for 2 weeks AND (2) sales team is converting 15%+ of calls to clients. Don't scale ads if the sales conversion is broken — fix sales first.

Scale decision framework

Upgrade from ₹300 → ₹500/day when: CPL is under ₹600 for 10+ days AND you've closed at least 1 client from ad leads. Upgrade from ₹500 → ₹800+/day when: CPL is under ₹500 consistently, 2+ clients closed from ads, and your sales team can handle 15+ calls/month.
